About Me
I enjoy learning and tinkering with electronics and circuits, as well as applying computational molecular dynamics to study nanoscale systems. I also simply enjoy coding projects that others can benefit from in my free time.
During the summer of 2025, I had the opportunity to do paid research at the Mechanical Engineering Department at Rowan, studying the effects of defects on lattice thermal conductivity via molecular dynamics simulations. In the previous year, I worked as a lab intern under the Laboratory Learning Experience at Princeton (LLP). There, I studied plasma-assisted catalysis of ammonia synthesis using WO₃ by uncovering and calculating the energetics of different species' reaction pathways. Learn more about my experiences here!
I've worked on many projects in the past, all of which have helped me develop valuable interdisciplinary skills. I am currently working on several PCBs for the Illinois Space Society's Spaceshot team, including a live-video camera board as well as a power distribution and ground station board. Previously, I designed and iterated through multiple revisions of a custom flight-control-system PCB for my team's model rocket competing in The American Rocketry Challenge (TARC).
In addition to my hardware experience, I have built full-stack software projects using Flutter, Python, and Go. One such project - Genibook - was a full-stack application (built before LLMs became popular, and StackOverflow was the norm) that authenticated users against my school's official gradebook (Genesis), scraped and aggregated their academic data, and presented it through a significantly more intuitive and feature-rich user interface.
Moreover, I enjoy competing in hackathons for their fast-paced, innovation-driven environment, and several of my projects have earned top placements. You can view a full list of my projects, both large and small, here.
While I am heavily technically focused, I'm a real human and love using my free time for other things too! I love listening to music, enjoy learning about birds, trees, and bugs (happy to chat if you're curious 😄), and playing wiffleball with friends. Of all the places I'd like to visit, Switzerland is at the top of my list - hopefully soon.
